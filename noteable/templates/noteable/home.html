Our Product

Noteable is an instrument practice tool for beginner violin players. Its easy-to-use interface makes it perfect for musicians of all ages. To start, simply click the Login button on the upper right-hand of the screen and login or create an account if you are new. You can then upload any piece of sheet music or choose from the many in our violin music library. Set the metronome tempo to that which you would like to play. Then, record yourself playing the music directly from whatever device you are using (laptop, phone, etc). When you are finished, Noteable will highlight any missed notes in red, showing which sections need more practice! A final percentage will be displayed, indicating how correct you performed the piece. Save sheet music and recordings to your account so you can come back and continue practicing at any time.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. 1. Is the practice tool free?
Yes! Access to the practice tool as well as our library of beginner violin music is free and accessible by simply registering for an account.
2. How do I register for an account?
Login here if you have already created an account, otherwise select ‘Register’ from the Login page. Registration is quick and easy, requiring only an email.
3. Why do I need to register for an account?
An account is required to access the practice tool so that you can come back later and keep practicing the same music. Uploaded sheet music will be saved to your account so that you don’t have to re-upload the music every time! You can also track your progress by viewing performance percentages over time.
4. Can I use the tool if I am not a beginner?
Yes, anyone can use our practice tool. However, due to the limited amount of time available to create the product, the tool can only recognize notes that are one of the following: whole note, half note, quarter note, or eighth note. Because of this, beginners are most likely to not encounter notes of greater complexity.
5. Can this tool be used with other instruments?
This tool is designed specifically for the violin, considering the most common range of notes for a beginner player as well as tailoring our pitch and rhythm formulas to that of a violin player. Different audio inputs will still create a conversion, but results will become less accurate as one moves further from a violin sound (i.e. a cello will be more accurate than a trumpet).
6. How accurate are my results?
Music transcription is an ongoing field of research, so we do not claim our tool to be 100% accurate. The current accuracy for related works in the field is 90-95%, so our tool will be as close to this as possible. Also, our sheet music conversion from pdf to xml has an accuracy of at least 98%; manual conversion is available to correct mistakes. More details on the definition of accuracy can be found in our Design Report.
7. How is my input audio transcribed?
Audio transcription is converted using onset pitch detection. Rhythm detection is calculated by combining the length between notes with the input user tempo. All of this data is sent through AWS to create the visual you see output in your browser!
8. Can I recreate this tool?
Yes! All steps taken to create this website and back-end can be found on our WordPress Website. Some expenses may be required to follow the exact same steps that we did, but workarounds are available! We’d love to hear feedback if you try to follow our path!
